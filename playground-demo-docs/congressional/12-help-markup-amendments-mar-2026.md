# Senate HELP Committee -- Markup of S.1847, Insulin Price Cap Act

**Date:** March 20, 2026
**Time:** 10:00 AM -- 4:47 PM
**Location:** 430 Dirksen Senate Office Building, Washington, D.C.
**Committee:** Senate Committee on Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions (HELP)
**Presiding:** Chairman Maria Santos (D-NV)

---

## Call to Order and Opening Remarks

Chairman Santos called the markup to order at 10:02 AM. Twenty-two of the committee's twenty-two members were present. The committee clerk confirmed a quorum.

**Chairman Santos:** "This committee meets today to consider S.1847, the Insulin Price Cap Act, introduced by our colleagues Senator Whitfield and Senator Delgado. This bill addresses one of the most urgent healthcare challenges facing American families. Over 8.4 million Americans depend on insulin to survive, and too many of them are forced to choose between their medication and their other basic needs. This committee has held two hearings on this legislation, heard from patients, physicians, economists, and industry representatives, and today we proceed to markup. I want to thank Senator Delgado for her work in crafting a bipartisan substitute amendment that reflects input from members on both sides of the aisle. I now recognize Senator Delgado."

**Senator Elena Delgado (D-NM):** "Thank you, Madam Chairman. I want to begin by acknowledging that this legislation addresses a real crisis that transcends party lines. In New Mexico, I have met with hundreds of constituents who struggle to afford insulin. This is not a Democratic issue or a Republican issue. This is a human issue.

"Over the past four months, I have worked with colleagues on both sides of the aisle to develop a substitute amendment that preserves the core patient protections of S.1847 while addressing legitimate concerns raised by Republican members and by the pharmaceutical industry. I believe the substitute represents a genuine compromise -- one that protects patients, preserves innovation incentives, and includes accountability mechanisms to ensure the legislation achieves its intended goals. I will describe the substitute amendment in detail when it is called up."

## Consideration of Amendments

### Amendment 1: Delgado Bipartisan Substitute Amendment (Delgado Amendment No. 1)

Senator Delgado introduced the bipartisan substitute amendment, which replaces the full text of S.1847 as introduced.

**Senator Delgado:** "Madam Chairman, I offer my substitute amendment, which has been co-signed by Senators Whitfield, Hoffman, and Moreau. The substitute makes four principal changes to S.1847 as introduced.

"First, phased implementation. Rather than an immediate $35 copayment cap, the substitute phases in the cap over three years. In Year 1, the cap is $75 per month. In Year 2, $50 per month. In Year 3 and beyond, $35 per month. For uninsured patients, the manufacturer discount program phases from $100 to $75 over two years. This phased approach gives manufacturers, insurers, and PBMs time to adjust their business models and supply chain arrangements.

"Second, manufacturer R&D tax credit. The substitute creates a 15% tax credit for qualifying insulin R&D expenditures, defined as costs associated with Phase I through Phase III clinical trials for novel insulin formulations, delivery systems, and biosimilars. This credit is designed to partially offset the revenue impact of the price cap and preserve innovation incentives. The Joint Committee on Taxation estimates the credit will provide approximately $1.8 billion in manufacturer tax relief over 10 years.

"Third, a five-year sunset clause. The price cap provisions of the bill expire five years after enactment unless reauthorized by Congress. This ensures that Congress revisits the legislation to assess its impact on patient access, manufacturer R&D, and the insulin market. The PBM transparency provisions and manufacturer reporting requirements do not sunset, as they serve an ongoing informational purpose regardless of the price cap's status.

"Fourth, the substitute incorporates technical corrections to the enforcement provisions and adds a GAO study requirement. The Government Accountability Office will report to Congress within three years of enactment on the Act's impact on insulin prices, patient access, manufacturer R&D investment, insurance premiums, and PBM practices.

"I believe this substitute represents the most viable path to bipartisan action on insulin affordability. I urge my colleagues to support it."

**Senator Margaret Whitfield (D-WA):** "Madam Chairman, as the lead sponsor of S.1847, I want to express my strong support for Senator Delgado's substitute. I will be candid -- my preference would have been an immediate $35 cap. Patients cannot wait three years for relief. But I recognize that a bipartisan bill has a far greater chance of becoming law than a partisan one. Senator Delgado has crafted a substitute that achieves the fundamental goal -- a $35 insulin cap -- while building the coalition necessary for passage. I also want to note that the CBO scored the original bill at $14.2 billion in savings over 10 years. While the revised score for the substitute is still pending, I am confident it will show substantial savings that more than justify the modest R&D tax credit.

"I would also note that we have heard from the Finance Committee. This bill must pass both this committee and Finance to reach the floor. A strong bipartisan vote here sends a signal to our Finance Committee colleagues that this legislation has broad support. I urge all members to support the Delgado substitute."

**Debate on the Delgado Substitute:**

*Sen. Rebecca Goldstein (D-CT):* "I support the substitute. The phased approach is reasonable, and the sunset clause ensures accountability. But I want to register my view that five years is sufficient time to assess the law's impact, and I will oppose any effort to weaken the cap amounts during the phase-in period."

*Sen. Andrew Fletcher (R-WY):* "I appreciate Senator Delgado's effort, but I cannot support the substitute. Government price controls are wrong in principle, regardless of the phase-in period. The R&D tax credit does not change the fundamental problem -- this bill tells private companies what they can charge for their products. That is not the government's role."

*Sen. Patricia Hoffman (R-ME):* "I support the substitute and I want to explain why. In Maine, I have constituents driving to Canada to buy insulin at one-fifth the price they pay here. That is not a functioning market. When three companies control 96% of the market and prices increase 287% over twelve years while manufacturing costs remain flat, the free market has failed. The Delgado substitute is a measured, time-limited response to a market failure, and I am proud to support it."

*Sen. Daniel Moreau (R-LA):* "I was initially skeptical of this legislation, but the substitute addresses my key concerns. The phase-in gives manufacturers time to adjust. The R&D credit preserves innovation incentives. And the sunset clause means this is not a permanent government takeover of drug pricing -- it is a five-year intervention that Congress can evaluate and extend, modify, or allow to expire based on the evidence. I will vote yes."

**Vote on Delgado Substitute Amendment:** PASSED, 15-7

Voting YES (15): Santos (D-NV), Whitfield (D-WA), Delgado (D-NM), Goldstein (D-CT), Torres (D-CO), Ramirez (D-AZ), Liu (D-OR), Hoffman (R-ME), Moreau (R-LA), Henderson (R-AK), Patterson (D-WI), Clarke (D-NH), Washington (D-MD), Velasquez (D-FL), Choi (D-HI)

Voting NO (7): Fletcher (R-WY), Graham (R-SC), Pearson (R-ID), Morrison (R-KS), Burke (R-AL), Simmons (R-NC), Novak (R-ND)

### Amendment 2: Small Manufacturer Exemption (Morrison Amendment No. 1)

**Senator James Morrison (R-KS):** "Madam Chairman, I offer an amendment to exempt insulin manufacturers with annual U.S. insulin revenue below $50 million from the price cap provisions. While I oppose this bill overall, if it is going to move forward, we should protect small and emerging manufacturers who are developing innovative insulin products and biosimilars. These companies operate on thin margins and cannot absorb the revenue impact that the three major manufacturers can. Exempting them will actually promote competition, which is the best long-term solution to insulin affordability."

**Senator Delgado:** "I am pleased to accept this amendment. It is consistent with our goal of preserving innovation and promoting market competition. Small manufacturers and biosimilar developers should be encouraged to enter the insulin market, not discouraged by regulatory costs designed for billion-dollar corporations."

**Senator Whitfield:** "I concur. The price cap is aimed at the three manufacturers that control 96% of the market, not at emerging competitors who are part of the solution."

**Vote on Morrison Amendment:** PASSED, 14-8

Voting YES (14): Santos, Whitfield, Delgado, Goldstein, Torres, Ramirez, Hoffman, Moreau, Henderson, Morrison, Patterson, Clarke, Velasquez, Choi

Voting NO (8): Fletcher, Graham, Pearson, Burke, Simmons, Novak, Liu, Washington (Senators Liu and Washington opposed on grounds the $50M threshold was too generous and could be exploited)

---

### Amendment 3: Extension of Price Cap to All Prescription Medications (Goldstein Amendment No. 1)

**Senator Rebecca Goldstein (D-CT):** "Madam Chairman, I offer an amendment to extend the copayment cap framework in S.1847 to all prescription medications, not just insulin. If a $35 cap is good policy for insulin, it is good policy for EpiPens, for cancer drugs, for HIV medications, for every medication that Americans cannot afford. This is an opportunity to address the broader drug pricing crisis, not just one piece of it."

**Senator Delgado:** "I respect Senator Goldstein's ambition, but I must oppose this amendment. S.1847 was deliberately designed to address insulin as a specific market failure in an oligopolistic market. Extending the framework to all medications raises entirely different economic and policy considerations that this bill has not been designed to address. I am concerned that this amendment would cost us Republican support and jeopardize the bipartisan coalition we have built."

**Senator Whitfield:** "I share Senator Goldstein's goal of broader drug pricing reform, and I intend to pursue additional legislation. But today, this bill is about insulin. Expanding the scope risks derailing the most achievable drug pricing legislation we have had in a decade. I reluctantly oppose the amendment."

**Senator Fletcher (R-WY):** "This amendment proves exactly what I have been saying -- the insulin cap is the thin end of the wedge. Today it is insulin. Tomorrow it is every drug on the market. Senators Hoffman and Moreau should ask themselves whether they want to be the Republican votes that opened this door."

**Vote on Goldstein Amendment:** FAILED, 9-13

Voting YES (9): Goldstein, Torres, Ramirez, Liu, Patterson, Clarke, Washington, Velasquez, Choi

Voting NO (13): Santos, Whitfield, Delgado, Hoffman, Moreau, Henderson, Morrison, Fletcher, Graham, Pearson, Burke, Simmons, Novak

---

### Amendment 4: Annual CPI Adjustment for Cap Amounts (Delgado Amendment No. 2)

**Senator Delgado:** "Madam Chairman, I offer a technical amendment to index the copayment cap amounts to the Consumer Price Index for Medical Care (CPI-M) beginning in Year 4, after the $35 cap takes full effect. Without this adjustment, the real value of the cap will erode over time due to inflation. The indexing is set at 50% of the CPI-M increase, so the cap rises with inflation but more slowly than general medical costs."

No debate was requested.

**Vote on Delgado CPI Adjustment Amendment:** PASSED by voice vote without objection.

---

### Amendment 5: Manufacturer Reporting on Executive Compensation (Torres Amendment No. 1)

**Senator Angela Torres (D-CO):** "Madam Chairman, I offer an amendment to require that the annual manufacturer price justification reports under Title IV include disclosure of total compensation for the ten highest-paid executives, including salary, bonuses, stock options, and all other forms of compensation. The American public deserves to know whether the prices they pay for insulin are funding R&D or funding executive pay packages."

**Senator Morrison (R-KS):** "This amendment is punitive and has nothing to do with insulin affordability. Executive compensation is a matter for corporate boards and shareholders, not Congress."

**Senator Whitfield:** "With respect, Senator Morrison, when a company raises the price of a life-saving medication by 287% while the CEO's compensation increases by 400%, that information is relevant to the price justification Congress is requiring."

**Vote on Torres Amendment:** PASSED, 12-10

Voting YES (12): Santos, Whitfield, Delgado, Goldstein, Torres, Ramirez, Liu, Patterson, Clarke, Washington, Velasquez, Choi

Voting NO (10): Hoffman, Moreau, Henderson, Morrison, Fletcher, Graham, Pearson, Burke, Simmons, Novak

## Final Vote on S.1847 as Amended

Following completion of amendment consideration, Chairman Santos called for a final vote on S.1847 as amended by the adopted amendments.

**Senator Whitfield's Closing Statement:** "Madam Chairman, I want to thank every member of this committee for their engagement with this legislation. Today we have an opportunity to take a meaningful step toward insulin affordability for 8.4 million Americans. The bill before us, as amended by Senator Delgado's bipartisan substitute, is a measured, responsible approach that phases in relief over three years, preserves innovation incentives through an R&D tax credit, and includes a five-year sunset to ensure accountability.

"The CBO has scored this legislation at $14.2 billion in savings over ten years. We have heard testimony from patients who ration their insulin, from physicians who watch their patients deteriorate because they cannot afford treatment, and from economists who confirm that the U.S. insulin market is not functioning competitively.

"I want to specifically acknowledge Senators Hoffman, Moreau, and Henderson for their willingness to work across the aisle. Their courage makes today possible. I urge a yes vote."

**Senator Fletcher's Closing Statement:** "For the record, I oppose this bill in any form. It establishes a precedent for government price-setting that will extend far beyond insulin. The amendment offered by Senator Goldstein today -- extending the cap to all medications -- was defeated 9-13, but it will be back. It will be back next Congress and the Congress after that. This is the beginning of government-controlled pharmaceutical pricing in America, and history will not judge it kindly."

**Final Vote on S.1847 as Amended:** PASSED, 16-6

Voting YES (16): Santos (D-NV), Whitfield (D-WA), Delgado (D-NM), Goldstein (D-CT), Torres (D-CO), Ramirez (D-AZ), Liu (D-OR), Patterson (D-WI), Clarke (D-NH), Washington (D-MD), Velasquez (D-FL), Choi (D-HI), Hoffman (R-ME), Moreau (R-LA), Henderson (R-AK), Morrison (R-KS)

Voting NO (6): Fletcher (R-WY), Graham (R-SC), Pearson (R-ID), Burke (R-AL), Simmons (R-NC), Novak (R-ND)

Note: Senator Morrison (R-KS), who voted against the Delgado substitute amendment and introduced the small manufacturer exemption, voted YES on final passage. His office subsequently issued a statement explaining: "While Senator Morrison continues to have reservations about the price cap approach, the inclusion of the small manufacturer exemption, the R&D tax credit, and the five-year sunset clause addressed enough of his concerns to support moving the bill forward for further consideration by the full Senate."

## Post-Markup Developments

**Press Conferences:** Following the markup, Senator Whitfield and Senator Delgado held a joint press conference in the Capitol. Whitfield stated: "Today the HELP Committee sent a clear bipartisan message: the era of unchecked insulin price increases is coming to an end. We now look to the Finance Committee to act." Delgado added: "This bill proves that Democrats and Republicans can work together when patients' lives are at stake. I am hopeful that Senator Richardson will schedule a Finance Committee markup before the Memorial Day recess."

**Industry Response:** PhRMA issued a statement within two hours of the vote: "While we appreciate the bipartisan effort to address insulin affordability, we remain concerned that S.1847's price cap provisions will reduce investment in next-generation insulin therapies. We urge the Senate Finance Committee to consider alternative approaches, including PBM reform and voluntary manufacturer programs, that achieve affordability without jeopardizing innovation." The statement was issued by Dr. Patricia Thornton, Vice President of Policy.

**Patient Advocacy Response:** The American Diabetes Association praised the vote as "a landmark step toward insulin justice." T1International called it "the most important day for insulin access in a generation." JDRF issued a statement urging the Finance Committee to "follow the HELP Committee's lead and move this bill to the Senate floor without delay."

**Finance Committee Signals:** Multiple media outlets reported that staff from the offices of Finance Committee members, including Senator Ryan Nakamura's (R-HI) health policy director Jennifer Walsh, attended the markup as observers in the public gallery. Walsh's presence was noted by several reporters and lobbyists and was widely interpreted as a signal that Nakamura's office is closely monitoring the bill's trajectory. A Finance Committee spokesperson declined to comment on markup scheduling but stated that "Chairman Richardson is in consultation with members on both sides about the appropriate timeline for committee action on insulin affordability legislation."

**House Companion Action:** Representative Chidi Okafor (R-GA), sponsor of H.R.3456 (PBM Accountability Act), issued a statement following the HELP Committee vote: "I congratulate the HELP Committee on moving insulin affordability forward. However, I continue to believe that PBM reform, not manufacturer price controls, is the most effective and market-friendly approach to reducing insulin costs for patients. The House Energy and Commerce Committee will continue to advance H.R.3456, which addresses the root cause of high insulin costs without the unintended consequences of price caps." Separately, Representative Sandra Nguyen (D-WA), sponsor of the House companion to S.1847 (H.R.2901), stated that she would seek an Energy and Commerce Committee markup of her bill in April 2026.
